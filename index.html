Overview

RNA-Pathology‚Ñ¢ is an open research initiative aiming to organize and standardize spatially resolved molecular pathology data, with a particular focus on RNA-based spatial technologies.

The project adopts a schema-first philosophy, treating spatial structure, biological annotation, and molecular measurements as first-class entities.

Why RNA-Pathology?

Spatial molecular datasets are rapidly increasing in complexity and volume, yet remain fragmented across formats, technologies, and analytical assumptions.

RNA-Pathology‚Ñ¢ addresses this by providing a principled, extensible schema and database architecture that enables reproducibility, interoperability, and cross-study comparison.

Database & Benchmarking

The Database component provides curated, schema-compliant spatial molecular datasets for research and method development.

The Challenge component hosts benchmark tasks designed to evaluate computational methods under controlled and reproducible conditions.

Benchmark tasks are provided under defined usage terms.

Current Status

  • Schema definition: active
  • Documentation: available
  • Data ingestion: limited pilot datasets
  • Programmatic access: planned
